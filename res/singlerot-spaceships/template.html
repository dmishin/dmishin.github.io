Spaceships in the Single Rotation rule

Below is the list of {patterns:d} different spaceships, supported by the Single Rotation cellular automaton. Of them, 205 were discovered by the random reaction method (catching spaceships, escaping from the random seed); in total 108720436 spaceships were caught and classified. Remaining spaceships were discovered using brute-force search.

The rule is “Single Rotation”, its code string is [0,2,8,3,1,5,6,7,4,9,10,11,12,13,14,15]. Under this rule, each 2x2 block that contains exactly 1 alive cell, is rotated by 90°, and all other block are left unchanged.

In order to see spaceship images, your browser must support SVG. Click the image or URL to open spaceship in the online simulator. Table columns are sortable, click on header to change sorting order.

The list of spaceships is available in several formats:

  • JSON library for the online simulator. To use it in the simulator, copy-paste it to the bottom text area on the “Library” pane of the online simulator, and click “Import from JSON”
  • CSV file.
  • Google documents spreadsheet.
